# Saatgut Logo Design Description

## Intent

The Saatgut logo should represent stored potential: a seed held in order, ready for the right season. It should feel useful, calm, and agricultural rather than cute, corporate, or eco-cliche.

## Core Concept

The logo combines three ideas in one restrained symbol:

- a seed
- a sprouting cotyledon pair
- an organizing envelope or packet form

The mark should suggest that Saatgut is both a place of storage and a place of living growth. It is not only about plants in the abstract; it is about managing real seed stock with care.

## Symbol Construction

### Primary Mark

Build the mark from a vertically oriented seed shape centered inside a softly squared packet silhouette.

- Outer shape:
  - a simplified packet or label form with subtly softened corners
  - stable, upright, and slightly taller than wide
- Inner shape:
  - a seed or kernel form centered in the lower half
  - above it, two small upward-opening leaves or cotyledons

The packet frame communicates order and cataloging. The seed and leaves communicate viability and future growth.

### Geometry

- Keep the packet silhouette geometric and calm.
- Keep the seed shape symmetrical or near-symmetrical.
- Let the sprout break upward from the seed without becoming whimsical.
- Avoid complex linework, soil graphics, roots, or extra leaf clusters.

The overall silhouette should remain recognizable at favicon scale.

## Symbol Meaning

- Packet frame: curation, storage, labeling, inventory
- Seed body: preservation, trust, material reality
- Emerging leaves: planning, cultivation, seasonal activation

Together, the mark says: "stored seed, organized well, ready to grow."

## Wordmark Direction

The wordmark should use a human sans-serif with modest personality and clear legibility. It should feel practical and established, closer to a trusted field notebook label than a glossy lifestyle brand.

Recommended characteristics:

- medium weight
- open counters
- slightly softened geometry
- no ornamental flourishes
- no condensed, hyper-modern, or futuristic styling

The wordmark should sit comfortably beside the symbol without overpowering it.

## Color Direction

### Primary Logo Colors

- Deep evergreen: `#183128`
- Olive accent: `#7f9b47`
- Optional moss dark: `#45633f`

### Preferred Applications

- Single-color evergreen mark for most usage
- Two-tone version where the packet is evergreen and the sprout/seed accent is olive
- Reverse white version for dark evergreen backgrounds

Avoid bright gradients, multicolor rainbow treatments, or glossy effects.

## Visual Tone

The logo should feel:

- quiet
- dependable
- cultivated
- materially grounded

It should not feel:

- overly rustic
- playful or cartoonish
- biotech or laboratory-coded
- minimalist to the point of anonymity

## Construction Rules

- Use consistent stroke logic if outlines are present.
- Favor filled shapes over intricate stroked details for small-size reliability.
- Maintain enough internal separation that the seed and sprout remain legible at small sizes.
- Preserve a stable vertical center line.

## Safe Area

Keep a clear space around the mark equal to at least the width of one cotyledon leaf. No UI border, text, or badge should crowd the logo.

## Small-Scale Use

For favicon or app-icon sizes:

- reduce the logo to the packet-plus-seed-sprout symbol only
- remove fine internal detail
- prioritize silhouette clarity over symbolism completeness

## Wrong Uses

Do not:

- use neon greens or synthetic eco palettes
- add extra leaves, roots, or vines
- place the logo inside a glossy circle badge
- stretch the packet shape horizontally
- round every corner until the symbol becomes soft and toy-like
- use a generic leaf-only icon with no seed or packet reference

## Summary

The Saatgut logo should look like a labeled place where living seed is held with care. Its symbolism should balance storage and growth, and its construction should remain simple enough to work from app chrome to printed seed sheets.
